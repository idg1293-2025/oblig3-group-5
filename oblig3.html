How does e-commerce affect the environment?

Welcome! In this interactive experience, you’ll explore how online shopping and global deliveries impact the planet, and what we can do to reduce emissions.

Click on the question marks to test your knowledge along the way!

How many packages are ordered globally every year via the internet?

"E-commerce currently accounts for about 3% of global CO₂ emissions, by 2050, emissions from deliveries could grow to 17% of all transport emissions if trends continue."

Now that we’ve seen how online shopping creates emissions, let’s follow a package’s journey — and see where most emissions actually come from.

"Last-mile delivery — the final step of delivering a package — accounts for up to 50% of a delivery’s total carbon footprint."

While trucks cause a large share of emissions from deliveries, airplanes are even more polluting per kilometer. Next, we’ll look at how air transport contributes to global warming — especially through international online shopping.

"Airplanes contribute around 2.5% of global CO₂ emissions, but when you include all climate impacts like contrails, aviation is responsible for about 3.5% of global warming."

"A single transatlantic flight emits as much CO₂ as an average person produces in a whole year of driving!"

But the journey doesn’t end in the air — your package still needs to reach your doorstep. In cities, delivery traffic is growing fast, creating new environmental challenges. Let’s explore what might happen in the future if we don’t shift to greener solutions.

"By 2030, delivery traffic is expected to grow by 78%, drastically increasing urban CO₂ emissions if no greener solutions are adopted."

Connection to the UN Sustainable Development Goals

Goal 11

Sustainable Cities and Communities

The project shows how increased delivery traffic in cities can raise emissions if we don’t take action.

Goal 12

Responsible Consumption and Production

This project raises awareness about the environmental impact of online shopping, especially over-ordering and returns.

Goal 13

Climate Action

It highlights how emissions from delivery transport and air freight contribute to global warming, and the need for greener solutions.

What can we do?

Small decisions, like ordering fewer items or avoiding returns, can have a big impact. Sustainable delivery options and awareness of emissions are key steps toward a greener future.
